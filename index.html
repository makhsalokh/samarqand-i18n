Registan Square

The magnificent Registan Square is the heart of ancient Samarkand, featuring three stunning madrasas from the 15th-17th centuries. This architectural ensemble showcases the finest examples of Islamic art and represents the golden age of the Timurid empire. The square is surrounded by Ulugh Beg Madrasah (1417-1420), Sher-Dor Madrasah (1619-1636), and Tilya-Kori Madrasah (1646-1660), each adorned with intricate tilework and majestic portals.

3 Historic Madrasas

Bibi-Khanym Mosque

One of the largest mosques in the Islamic world, Bibi-Khanym was built by Timur (Tamerlane) in the early 15th century. Named after his beloved wife, this architectural marvel once stood as a symbol of Timur's power and devotion. The mosque complex includes the main mosque, two smaller mosques, and a madrasah. Despite earthquake damage over centuries, restoration efforts have revived much of its former grandeur, with its massive dome and towering minarets still inspiring awe.

1404 Year Built
35m Dome Height

Shah-i-Zinda Complex

This stunning necropolis, known as the "Living King," is a sacred pilgrimage site containing the tomb of Qusam ibn Abbas, cousin of Prophet Muhammad. The complex features a magnificent collection of mausoleums built between the 11th and 15th centuries, renowned for their brilliant blue ceramic tilework. Walking up the narrow street between the mausoleums is like stepping through a corridor of Islamic art history, with each structure displaying unique architectural details and spiritual significance.

20+ Mausoleums
1000+ Years of History

Ulugh Beg Observatory

Built in the 1420s by Ulugh Beg, grandson of Timur and a renowned astronomer-king, this observatory was one of the finest in the Islamic world. The main instrument was a massive marble sextant, 40 meters in radius, used to observe stellar positions with remarkable accuracy. Though most of the structure was destroyed, the underground portion of the sextant remains, representing medieval Islamic astronomy's greatest achievements. Ulugh Beg's star catalog, compiled here, was the most accurate of its time.
