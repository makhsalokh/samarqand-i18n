Discover Samarkand's Historic Treasures

Bibi-Khanym Mosque

The Bibi-Khanym Mosque is one of the most important architectural monuments of Samarkand. Built in the early 15th century, it was once considered one of the largest and most magnificent mosques in the Islamic world.

Shah-i-Zinda Complex

Shah-i-Zinda is a necropolis in the north-eastern part of Samarkand. The complex consists of rows of refined mausoleums connected by narrow passages, famous for their stunning blue tilework and intricate architectural details.

Registan Square

Registan Square is the heart of ancient Samarkand, surrounded by three magnificent madrasas: Ulugh Beg, Tilya-Kori, and Sher-Dor. This architectural ensemble represents the pinnacle of Islamic art and architecture.

Ulugh Beg Observatory

The Ulugh Beg Observatory was built in the 1420s by the astronomer prince Ulugh Beg. It was one of the finest observatories in the Islamic world, where accurate astronomical calculations and star catalogs were produced.
